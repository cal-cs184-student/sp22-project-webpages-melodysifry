Part 5: Shaders

Explanation of what a shader program is and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects

A shader program, firstly, is a set of instructions, part of the graphics pipeline, that are sent to the GPU. They run in parallel. the vertex shaders are mainly involved in manipulation vertices in the 3d space, this includes both vertex positions and vertex attributes. These vertex characteristics are sent as inputs to the corresponding fragment program. The fragment is responsible for one of the last steps of the graphics pipeline i.e. to compute the color of each fragment and this is done using the vertex data provided by the vertex shader.

Explanation of Blinn-Phong shading model

The blind phong model basically divides the lighting of a point into three parts - diffused, specular and ambient lighting. Diffused lighting is that of general rough material (an incoming beam of light get reflected in all directions), specular lighting is the perfectly reflective component of that materialâ€™s appearance (rays of light follow the law of reflection and this gives the glossy, mirrory effect), ambient light is the general enivironment indirect light that lights up the space that particular vertex exists in (was largely created in this model to bridge the gap between reality and the addition of the other two, one could say that it tries to model indirect beams of light)
